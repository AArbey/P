Drancy Shoah Memorial


Introduction:

The memorial of the Shoah of Drancy is a complementary place to the memorial of the Shoah of Paris. It is a place of mediation between the site of the former camp and the public, a place of history and transmission.




Location:>

The Drancy Shoah Memorial is located in Drancy, 114 avenue Jean-Jaurès, opposite the city of La Muette.






History:>

La Cité de la Muette became in 1941 an camp d'internement, then in 1942 the camp de regroupement des Juifs de France en vue de leur déportation vers les camps d'extermination. Between March 1942 and August 1944, about 63,000 Jews were deported from Drancy camp, among the 76,000 Jews deported from France.





The memorial:

Built on land provided by the town hall of Drancy, this memorial was designed by the Swiss architect Roger Diener. The site of the Shoah Memorial in Drancy is sober and does not recall the history of the genocide; the Cité de la Muette is opposite and we see this sentence engraved: "Passant, recueille-toi et n'oublie pas" !
